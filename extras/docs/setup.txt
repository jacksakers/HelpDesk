The HelpDesk - Environment & Hardware Setup Guide

This guide covers getting your Arduino IDE ready to compile and flash code to your Elecrow 3.5" ESP32 HMI display, specifically focusing on the graphics, touch, and SD card functionality.

Phase 1: Arduino IDE Board Manager Setup

Open Arduino IDE, go to File > Preferences.

In the "Additional Boards Manager URLs" field, add this URL:
https://raw.githubusercontent.com/espressif/arduino-esp32/gh-pages/package_esp32_index.json
(If you already have URLs there, separate them with a comma).

Go to Tools > Board > Boards Manager.

Search for esp32 by Espressif Systems and click Install.

Once installed, go to Tools > Board > ESP32 Arduino and select ESP32 Dev Module (or the specific Elecrow WROOM/S3 board if listed in the manual).

Phase 2: Crucial Library Installation

To get the most out of this screen, you need a graphics library and a UI framework.
Go to Sketch > Include Library > Manage Libraries. Install the following:

TFT_eSPI by Bodmer: This is the fastest, most reliable driver for ESP32 TFT screens.

lvgl (Light and Versatile Graphics Library): An incredible open-source graphics library that gives you buttons, sliders, animations, and a modern smartphone-like UI.

ESP32-audioI2S (by schreibfaul1): Essential for playing MP3s from your SD card out of the speaker port.

Phase 3: The "Gotcha" - Configuring TFT_eSPI

Note: This is where 90% of people get stuck. TFT_eSPI needs to know the exact wiring of your specific Elecrow board.

Navigate to your Arduino libraries folder (usually Documents/Arduino/libraries/TFT_eSPI).

You need to edit the User_Setup.h file (or point it to a specific setup in User_Setup_Select.h).

Look at the Elecrow GitHub repo you cloned. They will have a specific User_Setup.h file or a block of #define code for their 3.5" board.

Copy the pin definitions (MOSI, MISO, SCK, CS, DC, RST) from the Elecrow repo and overwrite the ones in your TFT_eSPI/User_Setup.h file.

Phase 4: SD Card Preparation

Format your MicroSD card to FAT32 (ESP32 struggles with exFAT or NTFS).

Create a few folders on the root of the SD card to keep things organized:

/mp3 (for your music/sound effects)

/images (for background wallpapers or photo frame pics - preferably formatted as .bmp or .rgb565 raw files for fast loading)

/config (for future JSON files to save your to-do lists or settings)

Insert the SD card into the Elecrow display.

Phase 5: Your First Test Flash

Plug your Elecrow board into your computer via USB.

In Arduino IDE, select your COM Port under Tools > Port.

Important ESP32 Settings (under Tools):

Upload Speed: 921600

Flash Frequency: 80MHz

PSRAM: Enabled (If your specific Elecrow model has PSRAM, enable it. It's crucial for loading big images or running LVGL smoothly).

Open the Arduino IDE, go to File > Examples > TFT_eSPI > 320 x 240 (or 480x320 depending on your screen) > TFT_Bouncing_Box.

Click Upload. If the box bounces on your screen, your display driver is perfectly configured!
